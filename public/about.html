About Us

What is the HSQSA?

The HS Quantum Science Association (HSQSA) is an organization dedicated to spreading an interest in quantum computing and science amongst high schoolers. Although a growing number of high schoolers are interested in quantum science, the numbers are still very low due to the topic's inaccessibility for junior researchers; we aim to challenge the notion that quantum sciences are inaccessible by offering a variety of programs and opportunities.

Why was the HSQSA created?

The main objective of the HSQSA is to inspire the next generation of scientists in the quantum world by allowing students to start exploring such fields at the high school level. Many students are not even aware of the existence of quantum computers, much less their potential. Our organization will not only allow interested students to find the resources they need but also students who have never even heard of quantum mechanics to explore it further. As a result of the increased accessibility of quantum science to the high school population, more students will be motivated to continue exploring quantum computing at the undergraduate level and beyond.

Advisory Board

Prof. Mario Szegedy
Rutgers University
Mario Szegedy received his PhD in CS from the University of Chicago in 1989. From 1991 to 1999 he worked at Bell Laboratories. He became a professor of CS at Rutgers University in 2000, and his areas of research are computational complexity theory and quantum computing. He was awarded the Gödel Prize twice (2001 and 2005) and in quantum computing, the "Szegedy Walk'' is named after him.
Prof. Zheng Zhang
Rutgers University
Zheng Zhang is an Associate Professor of Computer Science at Rutgers University. Her research is in compilers, systems, and quantum computing. Her recent work dealt with the synergistic interaction between quantum applications, languages, system software, micro-architecture, and hardware. Her Erdős number is 2.
