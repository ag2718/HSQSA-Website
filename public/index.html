Helping Develop the Next Generation of Quantum Scientists

What is the NJQCA?

The NJ Quantum Computing Academy (NJQCA) is an organization dedicated to spreading an interest in quantum computing and science amongst high schoolers. Currently, not many high schoolers are interested in quantum science because of its inaccessibility for younger students; we aim to change that with the variety of programs and opportunities we offer.

Why was the NJQCA created?

The main objective of the NJQCA is to inspire the next generation of scientists in the quantum world by allowing students to start exploring such fields at the high school level. Many students are not even aware of the existence of quantum computers, much less their potential. Our organization will not only allow interested students to find the resources they need but also students who have never even heard of quantum mechanics to explore it further. As a result of the increased accessibility of quantum science to the high school population, more students will be motivated to continue exploring quantum computing at the undergraduate level and beyond.
